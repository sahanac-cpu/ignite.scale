# Setup steps (do this once)

1. Go to https://business.google.com → your Business Profile
2. Click "Get more reviews" → "Share review form"
3. Copy the short URL (looks like https://g.page/r/...)
4. Save that URL — that's your direct review link

# Template — text to send (WhatsApp / SMS preferred over email — much higher response)

Hi {Client name},

I hope you're well. Quick favour to ask if you have 2 minutes.

We're trying to make ignite-scale easier for other Dubai brands to find when they're researching agencies. Google reviews from real clients matter a lot for that.

Would you be willing to leave us an honest review on our Google profile? Even one line about your experience would mean a lot. Doesn't need to be a glowing essay, just honest.

Direct link to the review form (no login hassle): {YOUR REVIEW URL}

If we haven't earned it yet, I'd much rather know that than have you write a polite one. Either way, thank you.

— Sahana

# When to send this (timing matters more than the words)

Best moments to ask, in order of conversion rate:

1. **Right after they get their first qualified lead from us** — they're feeling the value, the ask is natural
2. **Right after their monthly report shows a metric they're happy about** — pin it to a specific win
3. **At month 3 of working together** — past the honeymoon, into real results
4. **At a renewal or contract extension moment** — they're already saying yes to us
5. **After they publicly praise us in any other context** (email, Slack, in-person) — strike while the iron's hot

Worst times to ask:

- During a bad week or a campaign that's underperforming
- Right before a renewal conversation (feels coercive)
- After we've asked them for anything else recently (referral, testimonial, case study)

# Do not say

- "5-star review" — Google detects this language and devalues the review
- "We'd really appreciate a positive review" — same problem, also a Google policy violation
- "We'll give you {anything} for a review" — Google policy violation, fineable in UAE

# Do say

- "An honest review"
- "Your experience"
- "Would mean a lot"

# Follow-up cadence

- Day 0: initial ask
- Day 4: gentle nudge ("just bumping this up if you missed it, no pressure")
- Day 10: stop. Don't ask a third time. The relationship is more valuable than the review.

# Realistic conversion

Out of 10 real happy clients asked this way, expect 4-6 to actually leave a review. That's enough to build to 20+ reviews in 6 months once we have a stable roster.
