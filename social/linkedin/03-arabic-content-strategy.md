# LinkedIn post draft

We audit a lot of Dubai brands' Meta accounts.

Roughly 9 out of 10 are running English-only creative in a market that's 30%+ Arabic-preference.

The CPL gap they don't know they have: 30–60%.

A few things we've learned from running bilingual campaigns in the UAE:

→ Bilingual ad copy (English headline + Arabic CTA, or vice versa) lifts CTR by 18–40% in our tests. Tiny investment, immediate impact.

→ Native Arabic UGC video outperforms dubbed/subtitled English UGC by 2–3x on engagement. The audience can tell.

→ Arabic landing pages are still rare among Dubai agencies. Even a competent Arabic page gives you an unfair conversion edge.

→ Machine translation is worse than no translation. UAE residents read it instantly. Spend AED 200–500 per ad set on a native Arabic copywriter. It's the highest-ROI spend in your campaign.

The Arabic-preference user isn't always the user with their phone in Arabic. Many UAE residents are bilingual but click whichever ad speaks to them first. If you serve only English, you're effectively self-selecting against half your market.

Most surprising data point from our last 12 months:

We A/B tested Arabic vs English landing pages on the same product, same paid traffic source, in 6 UAE campaigns. Arabic won 4 out of 6, and tied 1 out of the remaining 2.

The full playbook on what to translate, when to translate, and how to measure it: link in comments.

If you run a Dubai brand and you're English-only on your paid: what's stopped you from going bilingual?
