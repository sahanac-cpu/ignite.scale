# LinkedIn post draft

If your Meta campaigns target "Dubai, United Arab Emirates" you are setting AED on fire.

Downtown is not Marina.

Marina is not JLT.

Mirdif is not Palm Jumeirah.

These are not minor differences. The conversion rate gap between Downtown and Mirdif for the same product, same creative, can be 4x. The CPL gap can be 3x. The audiences are not even speaking the same cultural language.

Our team's quick map for Dubai paid geo:

🏙 Downtown — affluent expats, time-poor, respond to convenience and concierge messaging. Avoid discount language.

🌊 Marina + JBR — mid-career professionals, aspirational, couple/family-oriented. Lifestyle creative wins.

🏢 JLT — younger working professionals, dense SME activity, more price-conscious. Practical value beats luxury positioning.

🏨 Business Bay — mixed-use, transient residents. Quality-of-life services convert here.

🌴 Palm Jumeirah — concentrated wealth, ad-fatigued, only original editorial-feeling creative cuts through.

🕌 Jumeirah + Umm Suqeim — Emirati and long-tenure households. Arabic-first creative, trust signals, family-led messaging.

🏘 Mirdif / Al Quoz / Al Barsha — value-conscious mass market. Show prices. Be specific.

Most agencies set "Dubai" as the geo and walk away.

That's the easy choice and it costs their clients real money.

The hard choice is running 8–12 hyper-local ad sets with neighbourhood-specific creative. It takes more work. It also drops CPL by 20–40% within 14 days, every time we've done it.

Long version with the Meta and Google how-to: link in comments.

Which Dubai neighbourhood do you find easiest or hardest to advertise to?
